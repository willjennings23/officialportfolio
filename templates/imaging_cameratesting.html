002 : Camera Tester

As part of a larger quality control system I worked on as a co-op of Overwatch Imaging, an aerospace startup using AI to "find the needle in the haystack" from the air, I designed a setup to house and focus any of our production cameras. I first created a mount that could resize to clamp any camera body, and then I designed a focus mechanism to mount to the focus ring of any lens. Finally, to control the camera, I used a focus motor control board, camera interface boards, and a frame grabber.

Problem Statement

To test different cameras image quality and focus capabilities, cameras were not fixed in one place (i.e. handheld, or placed on a desk), making it difficult to focus on a single object (when pointing out the window) without shaking or movement. They would also be focused by hand, reducing precision and not providing a direct comparison to real payload functionality.

Objectives

- Create a mount that can adjust size to clamp on to any of Overwatch's production cameras

- Be able to drive any lens focus with a motor

- Control both the live camera feed and the focus motor using a laptop

- Make a way to consistently verify that a camera is working before installation

Actions

- Designed 3 iterations of clamps in SolidWorks to secure the camera. Used two large threaded rods with butterfly nuts to fit any size camera, and neoprene foam to prevent any damage to the cameras

- 3D printed slotted brackets to mount the focus motor, and have the ability to move in 2-axes to drive different lens focus rings

- Configured a motor board to control the focus motor through commands from a GUI

- Utilized a frame grabber and interface board to send live video feed to a laptop

Results

- A camera setup capable of testing any payload imager

- Testing camera quality with this setup is now a requirement before installing any camera into a customer payload

- Tester was used for categorizing new imagers for future payloads, and for studying image quality and focus capabilities of current production cameras
