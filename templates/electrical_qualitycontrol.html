001 : Overwatch quality control system

At Overwatch Imaging, an aerial imaging startup accelerating at a rapid pace, there was no way to ensure electronics were properly functioning prior to integration into advanced remote-sensing payloads. This resulted in major production setbacks when boards malfunctioned due to the complexity of fully built up payloads. Over the course of 3-months, I designed a testing system to verify over 30 different electronic boards (gimbal controllers, motor encoders, frame grabbers, camera interface boards, power supplies) and 8 different imagers, with the ability to be expanded for future components.

Problem

Payloads were experiencing recurring issues due to electronics malfunctions. Due to the large number of electronic components in the system, pinpointing the issue could delay production schedules by days, or even weeks. Upon further investigation, I found that electronics were being integrated into imaging payloads with little to no verification of their functionality. However, at the time, testing one board would require an entire built up payload, which was not economical or efficient.

Objectives

- Simplify the structure of payloads to test boards in sub groups

- Determine the necessary inputs and outputs of all electronics to verify their functionality

- Build a physical test station for the production team to use

- Create documentation on Confluence so anyone can use the system

Actions

- Meticulously studied all payloads over 1.5 months and developed a set of independent subsystems that can be applied to all Overwatch products

- Designed a camera tester that could fit and control the focus of any Overwatch camera, and a motor tester to verify encoders and IMUs

- Developed testing protocols for all production electronics by determining expected inputs and outputs under proper operating conditions

- Made a modular breadboard to easily swap out electronics and subsystems for other to create the perfect setup every time

- Created 15+ cables to connect to board inputs and outputs

- 3D printed 11+ mounts to secure and ground electronic components

Results

- Presented final testing system to entire company and trained a production staff of 6 on how to test with it

- Support for over 20 electronic boards, and 8+ cameras

- Successfully categorized issues on an electronic board within an hour instead of days through the use of independent subsystems of 3-5 boards rather than an entire payload of 20+ components

- Over 20 Confluence pages dedicated to properly using the system, as well as how to evolve it as products change
