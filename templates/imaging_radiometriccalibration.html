003 : Radiometric Calibration

In order to get the best imagery for autonomous object identification, properly calibrated imagers is a necessity. When one of our LWIR imagers was reading temperatures of warm bodies in the ocean at over 100ËšC, it was apparent that the calibration was significantly off. In order to improve the accuracy of the temperature readings and increase image contrast in the desired detection range, I got to work on a new calibration table for our cameras.

>>scroll for more<<

Problem Statement

LWIR cameras were utilizing a skewed temperature map, resulting in false temperature readings, potentially leading to mis-identification of warm bodies. For maritime search and rescue applications, this could result in low contrast and possibly missing a person in cold surroundings.

Objectives

- Generate a new temperature calibration map

- Perform a 2-point and radiometric calibration to optimize image output

Actions

- Utilized a blackbody in a humidity-controlled environmental test chamber at specific blackbody and ambient temperatures to generate pixel values at each data point.

Blackbody and LWIR camera in environmental testing chamber.

Calibration data testing plan.

- Used company software to extrapolate pixel values at test temperatures to cover a broader temperature range.

- Conducted 2-point calibrations to minimize faulty pixel values and dead pixels on the camera sensor.

Results

- Accurate temperature readings for maritime search and rescue applications

- Removal of dead pixels to prevent false object identification

Lessons Learned

- Principles of infrared range imaging

- How the calibration of cameras work
