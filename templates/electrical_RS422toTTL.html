002 : Printed Circuit Board design

During my co-op at Overwatch Imaging, I had the opportunity to work on designing my first PCB to resolve data transmission issues across all of our 6+ models of remote-sensing payloads. Given the chance, I quickly got to work learning how to be more adept in electrical engineering and electronics integration. Through the guidance of our senior electrical engineer, I developed skills in designing electronics schematics and PCB layouts, and put my electrical engineering knowledge to the test.

Problem

Long lengths of wires within payloads were highly susceptible to noise and electromagnetic interference, causing payload errors in high-intensity mission environments. Transmission of critical imaging and control data to the main processor in wires travelling through the core of a motor experienced information loss.

Objectives

- Eliminate interruption of data on way to the central system processor and back

- Able to function in both directions

- Design for manufacturability of 50+ units

- Withstand fluctuations in voltage and current

Actions

- Researched various transceiver and voltage regulator specification sheets on DigiKey

- Translated the data protocol from Transistor-Transitor-Logic (TTL) to RS422, which could then be into positive and negative wires. By using differential signaling, the data transmission would have improved noise immunity, and reduced EMI radiation.

- Performed electrical simulations to ensure all components functioned on 15V, and could withstand fluctuations of ~15%

- Learned how to create professional electronics schematics on Altium CoDesigner

- Designed a Printed Circuit Board based on the schematic following manufacturing standards

Final PCB layout diagram

Results

- Manufactured 50+ boards to be implemented into 25+ future aerial imaging payloads

- Successful integration into all payload models

- Eliminated noise and electromagnetic interference, resolving data transmission issues
