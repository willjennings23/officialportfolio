003 : TK-8 Demo Model

During my co-op at Overwatch Imaging, I used my accumulation of product development skills to create a marketing asset to bring in new customers, and to train existing ones. I learned how to integrate PID gimbal controllers and motor encoders, and how to design a mechatronic system with strict voltage and current requirements.

Problem Statement

Due to the nature of the payloads created at the company, it was not possible to allocate a product for trade shows, customer training, or for aircraft fit checks - making it difficult to display the engineering happening at Overwatch Imaging to the outside world.

Objectives

- Create a model that acts exactly like a real payload

- Make the device startup as simple as possible for users

- Work within a specific budget

- Reduce weight of the original metal payload to be brought as carry-on for flights

- Design for multiple devices being produced in the future, document the assembly

Actions

- Redesigned metal components to function as parts 3D-printed in SLS nylon to both reduce weight and cost. Parts were then sanded and painted matte black to look indistinguishable from the real payload.

- Designed an electronic system utilizing a gimble controller, encoder, motor, and Arduino UNO to function as an input for triggering commands on boot-up

- Calculated the required voltage and current to run the 5 electronics, and then selected the proper power supplies to distribute to the system

- Utilized a PID gimbal control GUI to code a script that would simulate real payload functionality, and Arduino to create code that acts as a virtual button press to control the gimbal stabilizer.

- Fabricated a custom Pelican case to protect the model during transportation and to hold an international power adapter for overseas customers and shows

Results

- Performs steps exactly like the real aerial imaging payload to mimic collecting swaths of imagery data

- Showcased in 3 different countries, multiple industry trade shows

- Served as a fit check for a specific customer aircraft - which provided information that drove future payload design decisions and size constraints

Lessons Learned

- Electronic system design for motor control

- Gimbal stabilization

- Mechanical design for cost and weight reduction
