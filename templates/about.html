02 : About me

Who am I?

My name is William Joseph Jennings and I'm a Class of 2023 mechanical engineer with a concentration in product development at Northeastern University. My passion for engineering lies within remote sensing for defense and aerospace applications.

The best way to sum up my work style is that I put blood, sweat, and cheer into everything I do.

My favorite part about engineering is the problem solving. I believe the best way to live life is through challenging myself and seeing what I'm capable of. Through my experience thus far, I've found that the long and tough road almost always leaves the biggest smile on my face, and always makes me a better person. That is why I've chosen to be a mechanical engineer, and my goal is to bring that passion to every team I am a part of in the future.

Outside of engineering, I am an avid adventure photographer - and I take every opportunity I can get to hit the road and hike with my Canon. During my time in the Pacific Northwest I spent my weekends travelling solo to places like Juneau, Alaska and through the Central Oregon High Desert to work on my photography and explore. An important thing about me is that I'm always up for a new experience, no matter how out-there it may be!

Professional work experience

Overwatch Imaging

In 2022, I pushed myself to the next level. From July to December, I moved to Hood River, Oregon to work at a rapidly growing aerospace start-up called Overwatch Imaging as a remote sensing payload design co-op. The products I worked on designing utilized artificial intelligence and machine learning to recognize objects of interest from the air.

These payloads were mounted on the bottom of small aircraft and functioned in critical search and rescue missions, wildfire mapping, and in many other intense operations. As a passionate co-op at a company with no shortage of work to do, I quickly advanced my skills, and got my hands on high-level projects.

I worked on projects such as weather-proofing a payload through gasketing, designing printed circuit boards to eliminate electromagnetic interference during data transmission, and I created an entire quality control system for over three dozen different electronic boards and imagers.

Werfen/Instrumentation Laboratory
